We operationalize a falsifiable account of transformer behavior via displacement vector norms and linear separability (AUC) across the Verbose ↔ Concise behavioral transition in Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct. Three independent quantization runs reveal a bifurcated geometry: maximum separability at Layer 6 (AUC = 1.0 exact) and maximum norm at Layer 26 (~172, canonical), a ~47× amplification. A fourth run on the same-architecture base model (Qwen2.5-7B, un-tuned) using an identical protocol acts as a null-model control. The base model retains the Layer 26 norm peak but loses the Layer 6 separability focal point entirely, with dramatically lower Layer 26 norms (~73 vs ~172 canonical) and AUC degradation at middle layers absent in the instruct model. These results indicate the bifurcated topology is an instruction-tuning-specific property, not an architectural artifact — partially satisfying Criterion A (null-model parity) while sharpening the scope of the primary claim.

§ I

The Methodological Boundary Condition

Current mechanistic interpretability frequently relies on "circuits" and "features"—abstractions that often fail to provide object identity. If two researchers can observe the same subgraph and derive different mental models of the underlying computation without an experimental way to adjudicate between them, the framework is not scientific; it is hermeneutic.

"Any claim about a transformer that cannot be expressed in front-to-back transformer dynamics is not an explanation of a transformer."

To satisfy this, we must move from storytelling to system identification. The base model comparison introduced in this version allows us to distinguish properties of the learned flow from properties of the architecture.

Both models show monotonic growth to Layer 26. The instruct model's trajectory is substantially steeper, indicating that instruction tuning amplifies the architectural gain structure rather than creating it from scratch. Notably, the base model's Layer 6 norm is slightly larger than the instruct model's (4.3 vs 3.65) — the divergence accumulates through intermediate layers, not at the entry point.

The word_prepend Inversion

The instruct model's most anomalous family (word_prepend, AUC degrading to ~0.87 at Layer 26) becomes the best-behaved family at depth in the base model (AUC 0.996 at Layer 26). Meanwhile, canonical — the most stable family in the instruct model — shows the sharpest non-monotonic profile in the base (trough at Layer 11, AUC 0.954).

A plausible interpretation: instruction tuning trains the model to route full phrase-level instructions ("Answer verbosely.") through early layers efficiently. A bare positional token ("verbose" at position 0) requires deep-layer processing to resolve in the instruct model — but in the base model, which has no concept of following instructions, the positional token is as good a cue as any. This is worth testing by examining attention patterns at Layer 26 for the two conditions.

§ VI

Criterion A: Partial Satisfaction

Criterion A stated: "If a base or random-weight model shows the same v* norm explosion at Layer 26, the result is an artifact of architecture, not learning."

Outcome: Partially satisfied. The base model retains the Layer 26 norm peak, which could indicate architectural bias. However, three observations limit this interpretation: (1) the base model's Layer 26 canonical norm is ~72.8 vs ~172.5 for the matched instruct run — a 2.4× difference that is substantial and systematic; (2) the base model's amplification ratio (~17×) is substantially less than the instruct model's (~47×), inconsistent with a pure architectural artifact which would produce similar ratios; (3) the bifurcated AUC topology (Layer 6 focal point) is entirely absent in the base model. A full null-model test requires a random-weight model, which would be expected to show no coherent peak at any layer.

Remaining gap: random-weight baseline needed to close Criterion A completely.

§ VII

Disconfirmation Criteria

Criterion A — Null-Model Parity

Partially satisfied. Base model shows same-layer norm peak but 2.4× smaller magnitude and no AUC focal point. A random-weight model is required to close this criterion fully.

Criterion B — No Conditioning Signal

Not satisfied (i.e., signal exists). AUC = 1.0 at Layer 6 for canonical/phrase-prepend across all instruct runs. The signal is strong and replicable.

Criterion C — word_prepend Mechanism

The inversion between instruct and base models sharpens the question: instruction tuning appears to differentially route positional-token prompts vs phrase-level prompts. Attention analysis at Layers 6 and 26 is the natural next step.

Criterion D — Full-Precision Replication

Current coverage: 4-bit NF4, 8-bit INT8, 8-bit INT8+FP16 (instruct); 8-bit INT8+FP16 (base). A BF16 or FP16 full-precision run on both models remains an open item.

§ VIII

Conclusion

The bifurcated geometry—Layer 6 separability, Layer 26 magnitude—is an instruction-tuning-specific property of Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct. The base model preserves the Layer 26 norm peak (consistent with an architectural prior toward late-layer amplification) but shows 2.4× smaller norms, ~17× vs ~47× amplification, no early AUC focal point, a non-monotonic AUC trough at Layer 11, and an inversion of the word_prepend anomaly. Together, these differences indicate that instruction tuning shapes the gain geometry in ways that are measurable, replicable across quantization regimes, and qualitatively distinct from the base model's representational structure.

The base model comparison partially satisfies Criterion A and upgrades the primary claim: this is not merely an architectural artifact but a learned property of the instruction-following objective.
