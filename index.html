§ I

The Methodological Boundary Condition

Current mechanistic interpretability frequently relies on "circuits" and "features"—abstractions that often fail to provide object identity. If two researchers can observe the same subgraph and derive different mental models of the underlying computation without an experimental way to adjudicate between them, the framework is not scientific; it is hermeneutic.

We operate under a strict methodological boundary condition:

"Any claim about a transformer that cannot be expressed in front-to-back transformer dynamics is not an explanation of a transformer."

To satisfy this, we must move from storytelling to system identification using the local geometry of the model's flow field.

Across all three quantization regimes, the optimal layers for both AUC and norm were identical:

✓ INVARIANT

Optimal layers (6 and 26) remained identical across 4-bit NF4, 8-bit INT8, and 8-bit INT8+FP16 activation conditions.

✓ STABLE

AUC = 1.0 at Layer 6 (canonical) held exactly across all three runs despite precision differences.

This confirms that the bifurcated topology is a load-bearing structural property of the learned flow, invariant to quantization implementation.

4. word_prepend Anomaly — AUC Degradation at Depth

The word_prepend prompt family (e.g. "verbose You are a helpful assistant.") exhibits anomalous behavior not present in other families: AUC degrades monotonically from Layer 9 onward, falling to ~0.87 at Layer 26. This is consistent across all three runs.

A plausible interpretation is that a single leading token carrying the verbosity signal ("verbose"/"concise" as a bare word at position 0) is processed differently in later layers than a full phrase instruction embedded in the system prompt. This deserves explicit investigation rather than omission.

§ IV

Disconfirmation Criteria

This framework invites falsification. If the following were true, these results would be invalid:

Criterion A — Null-Model Parity

If a base (un-tuned) or random-weight model shows the same v* norm explosion at Layer 26, the result is an artifact of architecture, not learning.

Criterion B — No Conditioning Signal

If singular value summaries (p>1, p≈1) do not differ between "Verbose" and "Concise" trajectories, the Gain Geometry claim is void.

Criterion C — word_prepend Degradation

If the AUC collapse in word_prepend at late layers reflects a genuinely distinct representational mechanism rather than a token-position artifact, the single-axis "verbosity direction" claim requires qualification.

Criterion D — Full-Precision Replication

Current runs cover 4-bit and 8-bit quantized models only. A BF16 or FP16 full-precision run is needed to complete the precision-invariance claim as stated.

§ V

Conclusion

Mechanistic interpretability must cash out in the mechanism. By focusing on displacement vector norms and linear separability across layers, we move away from the "circuit" ontology and toward a rigorous description of how transformers steer information through expansive and contractive subspaces.

The stability of the Layer 6/26 peaks across three quantization regimes—and exact AUC = 1.0 at Layer 6 for canonical and phrase-prepend prompts—suggests we are measuring genuine dynamical limits of the trained system. The actual amplification ratio (~47×) is substantially larger than previously stated. The word_prepend AUC degradation at depth is an open question that the present data cannot resolve.
